Ingredients

  • ~500 grams rice
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 1 litre chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • ~400 grams of beef
  • ~75 grams of baconlardons
  • 6 spring onions
  • 8 tbsp oyster sauce
  • ~600 grams of mange tout
  • Optional: red chili

Instructions

  1. Peel and finely slice 5 cloves of garlic
  2. Add 2 tbsp of butter to a medium sized sauce pan, once melted, add the garlic and simmer for 1,5 minutes.
  3. Add the rice and stir to coat in garlic butter.
  4. Add the chicken stock and put a lid on the sauce pan. Bring to a boil and bring down the heat to medium-low. Simmer for 12-14 minutes.
  5. Remove the pan from the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  6. Slice the beef in strips.
  7. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok, and add the lardons and sliced beef. Add a pinch of salt and pepper for taste. Let cook for 3 minutes.
  8. Chop the spring onion and add 3 quarters to the beef, cook for another 2 minutes.
  9. Add the oyster sauce and heat up, bring to taste with pepper if necessary.
  10. Bring a pot of water to the boil for the mange tout, cook for 3 minutes.
  11. Drain the water from mange tout, bring the mange tout and rice together in a wok and stir fry for 5 minutes.
  12. Add the rice to a bowl and spoon over the meat and sauce.
  13. To finish off, add the remaining spring onions to the bowl.
  14. Optional: Slice the chili and add to the bowl.
